Cleverly Dumb Inventions — Volume 1

Some things are not to be taken at face value.

By Kathy Li
July 06, 2017



How the trend originally got started remains a mystery. Perhaps it was when Japanese inventor and magazine editor Kenji Kawakami coined the term "Chindogu" back in the 1990s, which depicts "the art of inventing ingenious everyday gadgets that seem like an ideal solution to a particular problem, but are in fact useless."

Millennials in English-speaking countries might have only heard of a more modern, tech-infused version, popularized by the Stupid Hackathon. The Stupid Hackathon is basically “a one-day event where participants conceptualize and create projects that have no value whatsoever."

I have always had a weakness for "useless" inventions that may look dumb on a superficial level, but are indeed powered by thorough design process or even -- in some cases -- profoundly dedicated tech implementations.

After all, as Steve Jobs once said, "the people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do."

And getting your brainchilds (no matter how trivial) out there is a good starting point for bigger and better projects to follow. If nothing else, you would at least have that much more experience building things from scratch.

To celebrate the art of carefully designed and built things that are utterly "useless," the Silicon Valley Times is featuring an inaugural award presentation.

In this first volume, a total of three cleverly dumb inventions have been handpicked.

#2 -- Is your cat plotting to kill you?

If you are a fan of The Oatmeal’s, you may have already seen this storyline before. Matt Inman, creator of The Oatmeal webcomics, is nice enough to warn us of signs that our cat wants to murder us.

Matt has now launched an iOS app in collaboration with his developer brother, Nick Inman. With this app, you will be able to keep tabs on your cat’s intent any time of the day, anywhere you are.
